Info

Purpose

I have a problem with my minimap awereness.
At first I tried a simple solution. I took a sound and played it in loop every 5 seconds, every time I heard the sound I looked at the minimap. But after first 5 minutes I started to ignore the sound.
So I wrote a python script that took a folder of sounds and played them in customizable interval. That worked fine but I had a problem of finding good amount of short enough sounds. Also every sound was not equally loud. In the end I ended up with 5 or 6 sounds and it just took a little bit longer to start ignoring them too.
And it was a python script so if I wanted to share it, people would need to download potentionaly malicious code.
So I decided to make a web version of it. And I didn't want to code file handling operations to feed the page with sounds or provide only limited sounds I selected.
I needed to generate random sounds.

How it works

Plays sound every {interval} milliseconds for {duration} milliseconds.

The sound is generated using oscillator
You can select between 2 types:
Sine is more natural sounding
Sawtooth is more of a buzzing sound

How I use it

General advice is to look at minimap every 5 seconds.
That may be good if you are well trained but I tried to play the sound every 5 seconds and it was too annoying.
I set my interval at around 8 seconds. Also I use it more of a reminder and not a command. Should look, not a must look.
I try to look at minimap everytime there is a chance. When there is nothing to last-hit or after just last-hitting. When someone pings. When I am safe. And every time I hear the sound it reminds me to do just that. Not to look at the minimap immediately. If I have a second I'll look.

Be careful with the volume

The volume is usually loud enough even when low.

Fun stuff

Try setting interval to 100 ms and enjoy your own little robot. Also lower the volume before trying this just to be safe!
